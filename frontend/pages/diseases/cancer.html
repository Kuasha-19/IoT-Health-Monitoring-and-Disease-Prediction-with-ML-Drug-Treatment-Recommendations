Oncology: Pathophysiology & Molecular Therapeutics

Biological Overview

Cancer is defined by the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells. While normal cells follow a strict cycle of growth, division, and death, cancer cells evade these signals. This survival is often due to mutations in the cell's DNA, specifically within proto-oncogenes which promote growth.

Tumor Microenvironment and Angiogenesis

To sustain rapid growth, tumors hijack the body's resources through angiogenesis—the formation of new blood vessels. By creating their own nutrient supply line, cancer cells can grow larger and eventually gain access to the circulatory system.

Detailed Classification

  • Adenocarcinomas: Cancers that form in glandular cells, which secrete fluids like mucus or digestive juices.
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma: A cancer that begins in the lower layer of the epidermis, the outer layer of skin.
  • Multiple Myeloma: Cancer that begins in plasma cells, another type of immune cell that produces antibodies.

Multidisciplinary Management

Targeted Therapy

Drugs designed to target specific proteins or genes that contribute to cancer growth.

Liquid Biopsy

A non-invasive test that detects cancer cells or DNA fragments circulating in the blood.

Radiation Oncology

Using ionizing radiation to damage the DNA of cancerous tissue, preventing replication.
